(1) How has the SF median home price per square foot changed over the past 20 years?¶

Looking at just the median sold price per square foot for all homes of the zillow table, the average over all zip code areas of SF are extracted for each month. The available data range from April 1996 up to July 2016. It appears that over this 20 years period, the trend upward is quite significant. In April 1996, the average median sold price per square foot is just above 200 while in July of 2016, it was around six times that figure. From the time series plot, we can see clearly, the effects of the 2008 recession when the housing market took a dive and doesnt recover until around 2011- 2012. It seems also that since 2008 up until now, the variation has been quite erratic. Changes happen rapidly over shorter intervals compared to years prior to 2008 perhaps due to the housing market reestablishing itself. There appears to be seasonality and further analysis could be done.

def find_food(time, day):
    '''
    This function takes in a time and a day and returns a dataframe of the info. for available mobile food places
    Input: time INT, day Str (day of the week)
    Output: pd.Dataframe with cols. StartHour, Endhour, Vendor, Day, and Address
    '''
    conn = sql.connect('copysf.sqlite') #connect 
    day = day[:2] #substring to match in database
    #query to extract entries matching the conditions
    quer = '''
    SELECT DISTINCT
    StartHour, EndHour, Applicant Vendor, DayOfWeek Day, Address
    FROM
    mobile_food_schedule sche
    INNER JOIN 
    mobile_food_permits perm ON perm.permit = sche. permit
    INNER JOIN 
    mobile_food_locations loc ON loc.locationid = sche.locationid
    WHERE
    (Status = 'APPROVED' OR Status = 'ONLINE' OR Status = 'ISSUED')
    AND Day LIKE '%''' + "%s' AND %d BETWEEN StartHour AND EndHour-1" %(day,time)
    db = pd.read_sql(quer, conn) #to df
    conn.close() #close connection
    return db

Examining mobile food vendors and their schedule, the function find_food is built to make use of sql to find the address, schedule, and name of available vendors for a given day and time. The query involves joining all three mobile food tables to extract vendors who are actively selling. This means only certain permit status qualifies. q5 = '''
SELECT
RegionName zip, AVG(MedianSoldPricePerSqft_AllHomes) ave_med_price
FROM 
zillow
GROUP BY zip
ORDER BY ave_med_price DESC
''' #averaged over years, grouped by zip code regions

map = Basemap(llcrnrlon=-122.6, llcrnrlat=37.65, urcrnrlon=-122.3, urcrnrlat=37.85, resolution="h",
    projection="merc"
) #basemap obj.

map.drawcoastlines()
#shapefile for zip code separated regions
map.readshapefile("sfzip/geo_export_63e29a55-a573-4028-8f51-c560f9a3db05", "zipcodes")

df_poly = pd.DataFrame({ #df, values for drawing shapes
        'shapes': [Polygon(np.array(shape), True) for shape in map.zipcodes],
        'zip': [int(e['zip']) for e in map.zipcodes_info]
        })
df_poly = df_poly.merge(home_med_price, on='zip', how='left') #combine price and corresponding shape

cmap = plt.get_cmap('Greens') #color map, green shades
pc = PatchCollection(df_poly.shapes, zorder=2)

ax.add_collection(pc)

mapper = matplotlib.cm.ScalarMappable(norm=norm, cmap=cmap)
